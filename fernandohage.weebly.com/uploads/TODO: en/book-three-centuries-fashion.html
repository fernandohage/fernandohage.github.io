FERNANDO HAGE

THREE CENTURIES OF FASHIONS

ENGLISH VERSION - VER PORTUGUÊS
Published in 1923 in the city of Belém (PA) by João Affonso do Nascimento (1855-1924), it is considered a pioneering book on the history of fashion in the country. It was designed to commemorate the three-hundredth anniversary of the founding of the city of Belém, and before becoming a book, its drawings and texts were exhibited as an artistic exhibition in 1917.
​It presents the trajectory of clothing between 1616 and 1916, and in addition to writing, João Affonso designed 56 illustrations and the edition's cover itself.
As its author comments in the introduction, it is a set of “unpretentious pages”, a “look at the domains of clothing and its accessories during the period under retrospective examination”, which goes from the founding date of the capital of Pará, and he informs her that his drawings are based on documents he had. Its focus is mainly on figures of European nobility and icons of elegance from the history of fashion, but it is worth highlighting the author's last chapter, which is dedicated to three female “urban types”: the Preta Mina, the Crioula do Maranhão and the Mulata Paraense, very important records of the clothing traits of Brazilian society in the 19th century and a reflection of João Affonso's perspective as a caricaturist and journalist in Belém and previously in São Luís (MA), where he was born and lived until the beginning of 1880.
